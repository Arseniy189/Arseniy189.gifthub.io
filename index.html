Welcome to Bratsk! Located in Eastern Siberia on the shores of the mighty Angara Reservoir, Bratsk is a city that embodies the grand Soviet projects and engineering genius of its time. Founded in the mid-twentieth century, this industrial center has become a symbol of large-scale development initiatives during the era of rapid industrialization. History of Construction The construction of Bratsk began in the late 1950s as part of an ambitious hydroelectric project aimed at providing energy for the rapidly growing industry of the region. The main goal was the creation of one of the world's largest hydropower plants—the Bratsk Hydroelectric Power Station. This massive undertaking involved relocating residents from nearby villages, attracting thousands of workers from across the country, and establishing new industrial enterprises. The process of building the power station became a key event in the city's history. At that time, the Bratsk Hydropower Plant became the largest hydroelectric plant globally, producing enormous amounts of electricity. Infrastructure Features During the construction period, unique infrastructure solutions were implemented, making Bratsk stand out among other cities of that era: Housing Development Due to harsh climatic conditions typical of the Siberian region, special attention was given to constructing comfortable residential buildings. Many houses featured centralized heating systems, water supply, and sewage—features uncommon even in larger cities at that time. Transport Infrastructure To serve the vast construction site, new roads, railways, and piers were built. These developments significantly accelerated the delivery of materials and equipment needed for completing the work. Engineering Structures Of particular importance were the hydraulic structures ensuring the operation of the power plant. In addition to the dam itself, navigation locks were constructed to allow cargo transportation along the Angara River. Today, these unique facilities continue functioning and remain essential components of the regional transport system. Modern Bratsk Today, Bratsk remains a major industrial hub with well-developed aluminum production and wood processing industries. Despite being primarily focused on industry, locals take pride in their natural surroundings. Lakes, rivers, and forests create a breathtaking landscape that attracts lovers of outdoor activities and eco-tourism. A visit to Bratsk will immerse you in the atmosphere of Soviet industrial boom, allowing you to appreciate the scale of past engineering achievements while enjoying the unique nature of East Siberia.
